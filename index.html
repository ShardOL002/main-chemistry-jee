Why atomic radius changes across a period

THE CORE IDEA

More pull, smaller radius

Across a period, electrons enter the same principal shell while nuclear charge increases. The stronger effective attraction generally pulls the electron cloud closer.

Na→ Mg→ Al→ Cl

Remember: across a period, increasing effective nuclear charge generally means decreasing atomic radius.
